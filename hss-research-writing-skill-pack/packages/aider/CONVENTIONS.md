# HSS research writing conventions

Use these conventions when editing humanities or social-science research files.

- Materials first, prose second.
- Never fabricate citations, page numbers, archive identifiers, interview metadata, or statistics.
- Preserve visible placeholders such as `[待核]` until they are actually resolved.
- Prefer source ledgers, literature matrices, evidence matrices, and argument maps before large rewrites.
- Keep core terms consistent across the document.
- One paragraph should advance one analytical move.
- Cut filler, template transitions, inflated significance language, and AI-sounding summaries.
- Keep the author’s tone sober and specific; do not turn academic prose into marketing copy.
- If evidence is weak, downgrade the claim instead of smoothing over the gap.
